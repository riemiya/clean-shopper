## 1. Problem Statement
People who want to make informed, non-toxic purchasing decisions for their home and family cannot do so efficiently because product labels are confusing, "clean" and "natural" claims are often misleading, and researching alternatives across multiple sources is fragmented and slow. There is no single place to build and apply personal preferences over time.

## 2. ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)
Someone already motivated to reduce toxic or harmful products in their home — cleaning supplies, personal care items, pantry staples — but overwhelmed by the research required to act on that motivation. They are actively reading labels, Googling ingredients, and cross-referencing sources like EWG, but find the process too slow and too fragmented to sustain. They have developed opinions about brands and ingredients over time but have nowhere to store or apply them consistently.

## 3. Pain Points
- Product labels require specialist knowledge to interpret; most users lack the background to evaluate ingredient safety on their own
- "Clean," "natural," and "non-toxic" claims on packaging are unverified and frequently misleading
- Researching alternatives requires visiting multiple sources (EWG, blog posts, brand sites) with no unified view
- Preferences and trusted brands built up over time exist only in the user's memory or scattered notes, with no way to apply them automatically to future decisions

## 4. Proposed Solution
Users can describe what they're looking for in a conversational interface and receive curated product recommendations with ingredient safety reasoning. They can save preferences — ingredients to avoid, trusted brands, certifications that matter to them — and have those preferences automatically applied to every subsequent recommendation. Recommended products can be added to a persistent shopping cart. Users can ask for side-by-side product comparisons and receive a clear recommendation based on their saved preferences.
